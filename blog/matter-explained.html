What is Matter? The Smart Home Standard Explained

Matter promises to end compatibility nightmares in smart home tech. Here's everything you need to know.

March 25, 2026 6 min read

If you've shopped for smart home devices lately, you've probably seen "Matter compatible" on product boxes. But what does it actually mean, and should you care?

Short answer: Yes, Matter matters. It's the biggest improvement to smart home technology in a decade.

The Problem Matter Solves

Before Matter, the smart home was a mess of incompatible ecosystems:

  • Philips Hue bulbs needed a Hue hub
  • SmartThings devices needed a SmartThings hub
  • Some devices worked with Alexa but not Google Home
  • HomeKit devices often cost more and had limited options

You had to research compatibility before every purchase, and switching ecosystems meant starting over.

What Matter Actually Does

Matter is a unified standard backed by Apple, Google, Amazon, Samsung, and 200+ other companies. Here's what it guarantees:

🔗 Universal Compatibility

A Matter device works with ALL platforms — Alexa, Google Home, Apple HomeKit, SmartThings, and more. Buy once, use anywhere.

📶 Local Control

Matter devices communicate locally over your network, so they work even when the internet is down and respond faster.

🔒 Built-in Security

All Matter devices must meet security standards. No more worrying about cheap smart devices as security risks.

🎯 Simpler Setup

One QR code to set up any Matter device in any app. No hunting for brand-specific apps.

Which Devices Support Matter?

As of 2026, Matter supports:

  • ✓ Smart plugs and outlets
  • ✓ Light bulbs and switches
  • ✓ Thermostats
  • ✓ Door locks
  • ✓ Window blinds
  • ✓ Sensors (motion, contact, temperature)
  • ✓ Smart TVs
  • ✓ Robot vacuums

Not yet supported: Security cameras, video doorbells (coming soon)

Do You Need to Replace Your Devices?

Not necessarily. Many existing devices have received free Matter updates:

  • Most TP-Link Kasa/Tapo devices
  • Philips Hue (via hub update)
  • Eve devices
  • Nanoleaf lights
  • Many others

Check your device manufacturer's website to see if a Matter firmware update is available.

Should You Buy Matter Devices Now?

Our recommendation: Yes, when buying new devices, prioritize Matter support. It future-proofs your purchase.

However, don't throw out working devices just because they're not Matter-compatible. Most existing smart home ecosystems continue to work fine.
