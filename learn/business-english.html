Common Mistakes to Avoid

Learn from these frequently made errors in business communication

‚ùå
Avoid
"Please revert back to me."
‚úì
Use instead
"Please get back to me." or "Please reply."
Why?

"Revert" means to return to a previous state. "Revert back" is redundant. Use "reply," "respond," or "get back to me."

‚ùå
Avoid
"I am doing the needful."
‚úì
Use instead
"I am taking care of it." or "I will handle this."
Why?

"Do the needful" is outdated British English rarely used in modern business. More direct phrasing sounds professional.

‚ùå
Avoid
"Please find attached herewith."
‚úì
Use instead
"I have attached..." or "Please see the attached..."
Why?

"Herewith" is archaic legal language. Modern business writing favors clear, simple language.

‚ùå
Avoid
"I wanted to reach out to you..."
‚úì
Use instead
"I'm writing to..." or "I'm contacting you about..."
Why?

"Reach out" has become overused corporate jargon. Direct language like "I'm writing" or "I'm contacting" is clearer.

‚ùå
Avoid
"Let's discuss offline."
‚úì
Use instead
"Let's discuss this in person." or "Can we schedule a call?"
Why?

"Offline" is tech jargon that doesn't always translate well. Be specific about how you want to communicate.

‚ùå
Avoid
"Kindly do the same."
‚úì
Use instead
"Please [specific action]." or "I would appreciate if you could..."
Why?

Vague requests lead to confusion. Always specify exactly what action you're requesting from the recipient.
